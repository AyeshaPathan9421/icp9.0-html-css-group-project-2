Devgad Fort

Location: Devgad, Sindhudurg District

Historical Significance: Devgad Fort was built during the Maratha Empire to protect the Konkan coast from foreign invasions. The fort played a crucial role in safeguarding the trade routes along the Arabian Sea. It features a lighthouse that has guided ships for centuries.

Naldurg Fort

Location: Naldurg, Osmanabad District

Historical Significance: Naldurg Fort is renowned for its elliptical shape, a unique design among Indian forts. It was constructed by the Bahmani Sultanate in the 14th century. The fort’s strategic location allowed it to control the surrounding area and defend against invasions.

Paranda Fort

Location: Paranda, Osmanabad District

Historical Significance: Built by the Bahmani Sultanate, Paranda Fort was a key military outpost in the region. Its construction reflects the architectural style and fortification techniques of the Bahmani era. The fort was crucial in defending the northern territories of the Sultanate.

Fort of Belapur

Location: Belapur, Navi Mumbai

Historical Significance: The Fort of Belapur was initially constructed by the Marathas in the 14th century. It was later expanded and modified by the British during colonial rule. The fort served as a military outpost to protect the coast and manage trade in the Mumbai area.

Ambolgad Fort

Location: Ambolgad, Ratnagiri District

Historical Significance: Ambolgad Fort was built to defend the Konkan coast from maritime threats. Its strategic position allowed it to oversee the sea routes and prevent pirate activities. The fort’s remnants still reflect its historical significance in coastal defense.

Kanakdurg

Location: Near Sangli

Historical Significance: Kanakdurg Fort was used as a military outpost to monitor the surrounding regions. Its location made it a key point for controlling local trade and defense during various historical periods.

Bankot Fort

Location: Bankot, Raigad District

Historical Significance: Bankot Fort was initially constructed by the Marathas and later captured by the Portuguese. It played an essential role in the defense of the coast and control of the surrounding areas. The fort was used to manage maritime activities and trade.

Corley/Korlai Fort

Location: Korlai, Raigad District

Historical Significance: Korlai Fort was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century to control the maritime routes and defend against potential threats. Its architecture reflects the colonial styles of the period. The fort is notable for its strategic location overlooking the sea.

Bhuikot Fort of Solapur

Location: Solapur District

Historical Significance: Bhuikot Fort in Solapur was built during the medieval period and served as a defensive fortification. The fort's strategic location helped in controlling local movements and trade routes in the region.

Historical Significance: Bhuikot Fort in Udgir was an important fortification during the Maratha era. Its location allowed it to play a significant role in the defense of the region and control of local trade routes.

Dharmaveergad Fort

Murud-Janjira Fort

Location: Raigad District, Maharashtra, India

Built By: Siddis of Janjira

Year: 15th Century

Historical Significance: Murud-Janjira Fort is renowned for its strategic location on an island off the coast of Raigad. Built by the Siddis, it served as a powerful naval base and was key to controlling the surrounding maritime routes. Its impregnable position made it a formidable fortress against various invasions.
